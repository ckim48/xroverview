About

Project XR, in collaboration with the GIST VR Project at Carnegie Mellon University's Human-Computer Interaction Institute, strives to revolutionize mental health solutions through Extended Reality (XR) technologies. With a focus on combating self-critical and negative thoughts, the program pioneers tangible experiences that foster positive impact. By creating an immersive platform, the collaboration aims to provide users with innovative solutions, utilizing XR to enhance self-awareness and promote mental well-being. Join us in shaping the future of mental health interventions, where technology becomes a powerful tool for positive change, addressing the complex challenges of self-reflection and emotional well-being.

* The GIST VR Lab is an innovative lab in collaboration with Carnegie Mellon University's (CMU) Center for Transformational Play (CTP) under the lead of Professor Jessica Hammer. Professor Hammer, an expert in extended reality (XR), game design, psychology, and health technologies, has received prestigious awards such as the World Economic Forum Young Scientist award and the Okawa Award, with research support from organizations like the Heinz Foundation, Google, Amazon, and the NSF.

Team

Deliverables

Virtual Reality(VR) Team

The VR development team's objective is delving into the world of Virtual Reality (VR) by mastering the fundamentals of VR setting, development, and building within the Unity game engine environment. Through hands-on experience and collaborative learning, the team aims to become proficient in creating XR experiences that leverage VR interaction techniques, utilizing tools such as the Oculus Quest 3 device

  • Implementing Speech Technologies within the VR environment
  • Integrating ChatGPT with virtual avatars in the VR environment

UI/UX

The UI/UX team's obejective is to create design wireframes and a final prototype that simulate the VR experience within the constraints of a mobile interface. This project involves the complete design process, including conducting primary and secondary research, interpreting interviews, and developing multiple iterations of wireframes and prototypes. By providing a substitute for the VR experience, we aim to help users understand their behavior and effectively interact with the virtual environment. Ensuring usability and accessibility for all users, regardless of their access to VR technology, is paramount. We will optimize the mobile app's interface and functionality to achieve this goal. Additionally, we will incorporate feedback from users and stakeholders throughout the design process to refine and enhance the mobile app experience.

Aritifical Intelligence(AI) Team

The objective of the AI Team was to tackle the development of an XR-based AI Agent, leveraging models like LLM (Large Language Models) to understand human emotions through facial expressions and voice recognition. The team gained proficient knowledge in machine learning/deep learning and work with diverse models, ranging from modern CNNS to Transformers, and delved into the foundations of Machine Learning and real-life examples to illustrate the application of these techniques across various domains.

  • Working with a variety of models, including modern Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Transformers
  • Leveraging models like Large Language Models (LLMs) to understand human emotions through facial expressions and voice recognition

Computer Vision(CV) Team

The objective of the CV team is to create an emotion recognition model to detect the emotions of users viewing content on a website. The team aimed to test and analyze several machine learning models to select the best-performing one for emotion recognition. The input to the model will be video frames from the user’s camera, and the user’s information will be integrated with their current emotion and the content they are viewing.

  • Develop Emotion Recognition Model: Create a model to detect emotions from video frames.
  • Evaluate and Optimize Models: Test and optimize various models for best performance.

Software Engineering Team

The Software Engineering (SWE) team’s primary objective was to develop an interactive web application for exploring data collected from the VR platform. This involved establishing a centralized database for efficient data access. The team engaged in full-stack development, utilizing HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Flask for front-end and back-end components. Throughout the project, the team has developed proficiency in implementing web applications while also honing their collaborative skills through teamwork with other teammates. The team also engaged in sessions to best translate client’s use cases into functionalities of the web application.

Natural Language Processing(NLP) Team

The objective of the NLP team was to develop a visual representation of a conversational interface for communication with virtual reality characters. This involved equipping team members with the skills necessary for analyzing and extracting meaningful insights from stored chat data, including techniques such as keyword extraction and summarization.

  • Create a visual representation of a conversational interface tailored for interaction with virtual reality characters.
  • Master fundamental analytical methods, such as keyword extraction and summarization, to derive valuable insights from the stored conversations.

Data Science Team

The objective of the Data Science team's project was to construct a recommendation system by utilizing advanced data analytics methodologies. Initially, the team employed data analytics techniques to analyze datasets, identifying significant patterns essential for generating an initial recommendation system. Following this phase, the team focused on refining the system through extensive user testing. The aim was to thoroughly assess and fine-tune the effectiveness and accuracy of the recommendation system based on user interactions and preferences.

Gender Studies Team

The objective of the Gender Studies team was to explore and analyze the fundamental principles of gender roles in society while delving into emerging gender norms through Queer Theory, Intersectionality, and LGBTQ+ Rights and Identities. The team aimed to examine how these theories can be applied to virtual reality (VR) platforms and how users interact with and respond to these applications. The final deliverable is a research paper that showcases the extension of these gender principles within VR environments. Each user response gathered during this process will contribute to the development of more sophisticated simulations. The format of the technical writing report will be utilized to merge gender principles with VR platform functionalities.
